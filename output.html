Step 2: Create Your Asset List

Create a list of your assets details. These typically include:

  • Property Addresses
  • Bank Accounts Numbers
  • Brokerage Account Numbers
  • Insurance Policy Numbers
  • Cryptocurrency Wallet Number

Note: Properties held in joint tenancy, CPF accounts and insurance with beneficiaries are not distributed through a will.

Step 3: Find a Professional Service Provider

Some common companies that provides will-writing services includes:

  • SimplyWills
  • FortisWills
  • Legal Firms (e.g. PKWA Law LLC, Invictus Law Corporation)

Be sure to:

  • Verify the credentials of any legal professional you engage. Refer to Ministry of Law - Search for a Lawyer or Law Practice
  • Discuss fees and payment terms upfront
  • Check if they will provide witnesses

Step 3: Explore Online Will Writing Tools

You can refer to:

  • Silver Years Will Generator from OCBC - Free of Charge
  • WillMaker by SingaporeLegalAdvice.com - $89

These tools are free to try, so there is no harm checking if they suit your purpose.

Step 4: Finalising Your Will

To finalise your will, you will need to sign on the printed will with your witnesses together.

Step 5: Storing Your Will

  • Upload the instructions to locate the will in MyLegacy Vault and add your Executor as the trusted person. Trusted person will also receive notification when the death certificate is created.

          OR

  • Upload the instructions to access the will with the SAL Will Registry.

The physical copy can then be stored somewhere safe, e.g. in a safe at your home, or at a legal firm or will-writing service provider.

Step 5: Storing You Will

Consider storing your will in your home and pass a copy to your executor.

General Will Storage Advice:

  • The executor requires the original, signed will, not a photocopy.
  • Ensure that the executor is aware of the location of the original copy.
  • Avoid storing the will in a bank safe deposit box under your name, as access will be restricted upon your death.
  • A fire-resistant and waterproof safe is recommended for optimal preservation.

Step 6: Communicate with your Executors and Beneficiaries

Inform your executors about your will. Informing beneficiaries is optional, but doing so can avoid confusion and potential challenges, especially if multiple will versions exist.

Step 7: Regularly Review Will and Asset List

To help your executor identify all your assets, consider maintaining and regularly updating an asset list. You can also make use of MyLegacy Vault to store your asset listing and add your executors as trusted persons. You should also update your will when there are major life events and changes in your relationships.
